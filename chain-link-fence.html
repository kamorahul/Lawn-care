Our approach

How we install your chain link fence

A proven installation process that ensures your chain link fence is properly tensioned, level, and built to last for years.

1
📐

Layout & measurement

We mark your fence line, identify corner and gate post locations, and check for underground utilities before any digging begins — ensuring accuracy and safety.

2
⛏️

Post hole digging

Post holes are dug below Calgary's frost line to prevent shifting and heaving through seasonal freeze-thaw cycles — giving your fence a rock-solid foundation.

3
🏗️

Post setting in concrete

Terminal and line posts are set plumb and level in concrete. We allow adequate curing time before attaching rails and mesh for maximum stability.

4
🔗

Rail & mesh installation

Top and bottom rails are attached and the chain link mesh is stretched and fastened with tension bars and ties — properly tensioned for a clean, taut appearance.

5
🚪

Gate hanging & hardware

Gates are hung with heavy-duty hinges, tension bars, and secure latches. We test all gate operation to ensure smooth, reliable use before we leave.

6
🧹

Site cleanup & inspection

All waste and debris is removed from your property. A final walk-around ensures every section is properly installed and meets our quality standards.

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about our chain link fence installation services in Calgary.

How long does chain link fence installation take?
Most standard residential chain link fence installations are completed in a single day. Larger commercial projects may take 2–3 days. We'll give you a clear timeline during your free assessment.
What areas in Calgary do you service?
We service all residential and commercial properties throughout Calgary and surrounding areas including Airdrie, Chestermere, Cochrane, and Okotoks.
Do I need a permit for a chain link fence in Calgary?
In most cases, fences under 1.85 metres (6 feet) on residential properties do not require a permit. Commercial fencing requirements vary. We can advise on permit requirements during your site assessment.
Are you licensed and insured?
Yes, we are fully licensed and insured for all chain link fence installation services in Calgary. Your property and our team are fully protected on every project.
What gauge chain link do you use?
We use 9-gauge chain link mesh for most residential projects and 6-gauge for commercial and high-security applications. Heavier gauge means stronger, more durable fencing.
What is the difference between galvanized and vinyl coated?
Galvanized chain link is the standard silver-grey steel option — durable and cost-effective. Vinyl coated adds a PVC coating in black, green, or brown for extra corrosion resistance and a cleaner appearance.
How long will a chain link fence last?
A properly installed galvanized chain link fence typically lasts 15–20 years. Vinyl-coated chain link can last even longer due to the added corrosion protection. Low maintenance makes it one of the most cost-effective fencing options available.
